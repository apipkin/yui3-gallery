B

Berroa, Felix

Phone: 678-355-1473Email: felbere@hotmail.comWebsite: www.felixberroa.com
Bio:

"I began drawing and carving wood and common stones during my childhood in the 50s. From the beginning, it was faces, the human figure, self-portraits, or just a pretty girl who would catch my eye.

My later work had been realistic, but during the last stage of my studies at the Escuela Nacional de Bellas Artes, in the Dominican Republic, my work began to transform into what it is today.

My ability was forged by a team of some of the most recognized artists of the Dominican Republic and Puerto Rico.

In my art, "the lines" wish to be seen in order to more definitely mark the 'expressions' of our joy, dreams, and suffering. The idea of those 'small lines' was taken primarily from my work as an etcher. By observing the Old Masters of that discipline (Durer, Rembrandt, and others), I assimilated the sort of line that they used to achieve volume and shadow in their forms. I adapted to enhance the expressions, not only in the faces of my characters, but in their bodies.

My characters are mostly women and children because I dedicate my attention to those human beings considered in the society as "weakest". They are actually the most tender and beautiful, the givers and receivers of life.

Dillehay, Kyle

Bio:

Kyle Dillehay is a graduate of Auburn University and holds a M.F.A. in sculpture from Georgia State University. His public commissions include the North Springs Rapid Transit Station, a project funded by MARTA, and a sculpture in downtown Atlanta commissioned by the Chik-fil-A Corporation. He has exhibited his work at City Gallery Chastain, Washburn University in Kansas, and Johnson Atelier in New Jersey. Dillehay taught at Georgia State University and Kennesaw State University. In 2006, he relocated to Tacoma, Washington where he teaches sculpture at Tacoma Community College.

Bio:

Don Haugen focuses his work completely on figurative bronze pieces with the goal of honing the craft to perfection. Johnson, Gregory

Bio:

Gregory Johnson was born in Chicago, Illinois. His early training includes the Art Institute of Chicago and Bowling Green State University. Later, he won the Elizabeth Stein Award of Art Excellence Scholarship, and traveled to Illinois State University, where Johnson studied sculpture, painting, and art history. In 1981, he moved to the Southeast where he has created public art works in Cumming in Forsyth County, Blairesville in Union County, Blue Ridge in Fannin County, Dahlonega in Lumpkin County, Athens Classic Center, Fayetteville, Thomaston, Barnesville, and Gainesville. Other works are located in numerous museums, corporate and private collections throughout the United States, Europe, Costa Rica, and the Middle East.

L

Landesberg, Amy

Bio:

Amy Landesberg is an accomplished artist and a practicing architect. She received a Master of Visual Arts from Georgia State University in 1981, and a Master of Architecture from the Yale School of Architecture in 1991. Her work is in the collections of the High Museum of Art, MOCA GA, and the Hyatt Regency Hotel in Atlanta. She has taught architecture at Yale, Columbia University, Tulane University, Ohio State University, Georgia Institute of Technology, and in the Interior Design Department at Georgia State University. Landesberg has received many awards including SAF/NEA fellowships in Painting and Sculpture, and the Moulton Andrus Award for Excellence in Art and Architecture. As an architect, she has completed projects for Kiang Gallery, Beth Jacob Synagogue, and several private homes.

Rittenberry, Harold

Bio:

Harold Rittenberry is a renowned self-taught artist based in Athens, Georgia. He has been exhibiting his work since 1994 and participated in exhibitions held throughout the South, including a solo exhibition, "Spirit Yard," at the Georgia Museum of Art in Athens. Rittenberry was one of twelve artists featured in the Folk Art Park commissioned for the 1996 Olympic Games by the Corporation of Olympic Development in Atlanta. Rittenberry also exhibited in Celebrating the Creative Spirit: Contemporary Southeastern Furniture, a traveling exhibition organized by the Mobile Art Museum that toured six southeastern museums between 1998 and 2001. He collaborated with artist Robert Clements on another Fulton County commission installed at East Point Library.
